The **Appointments** page shows your clinic's schedule. Every appointment links a
patient, a provider, a date and time, and a visit type.

## Working the schedule

### Booking an appointment

1. Open **Appointments** in the sidebar and click **New appointment**.
2. Search the **patient** by name or file number — the picker is keyboard-friendly
   (arrow keys to move, **Enter** to select). Their name and initials fill in
   automatically.
3. Choose the **date**, **time**, **type** of visit, and the **provider** — the
   provider is a searchable list of the clinic's doctors (arrow keys + Enter).
4. Save. The appointment appears in the schedule.

### Managing the day

- **Click any appointment** to open its detail panel, where you can edit the
  date, time, type, provider, and status — for example to mark it checked-in,
  completed, or cancelled.
- **Delete** (from the same panel) removes it from the schedule entirely (use
  status changes for normal bookkeeping; delete is for mistakes).

### Calendar view

The **Calendar** button (next to the search box) opens a Google-Calendar-style month
grid — color-coded chips per day; click a day to list its appointments in full. When you
ask the **AI chat** about appointments (e.g. "what's on this week?"), it replies with a
**condensed card** and a **View in calendar** link that opens this month view on the right
date, rather than flooding the chat with a card per appointment.

<Callout type="info">
  Appointments added from the AI chat show an **Added by AI** badge and may have
  placeholder fields (e.g. provider "Unassigned") — click the row to review and
  fill them in.
</Callout>

Reception, doctors, members, admins, and owners can all create and manage
appointments — scheduling is front-desk work by design. Pharmacy and lab staff can
see the schedule but not change it.

<Callout type="info">
  Changes to appointments show up in the [activity feed](/docs/guides/activity-analytics)
  and trigger notifications, so the team stays in sync without anyone sending a message.
</Callout>
