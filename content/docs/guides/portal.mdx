The **Patient Portal** is a standalone, full-screen page meant for a **clinic kiosk** — an
iPad or self-service device in the waiting room. It has **no sidebar and no login**: it's a
calm, large-touch flow for patients to book a visit or check on their results themselves.

## Opening the kiosk

The portal lives at:

```text
/portal/{clinic}
```

where `{clinic}` is your clinic's **slug**. Open that page on the kiosk device — it shows
your clinic name and two big choices.

## What a patient can do

On the landing screen the patient picks one of two cards:

- **Book an appointment** — they enter their **name** and **file number**, choose a
  **date and time** (past dates are blocked) and an optional reason, then submit. Once
  it's confirmed, the appointment shows up on your clinic's
  [Appointments](/docs/guides/appointments) page like any other booking.
- **View my results** — they enter their **name** and **file number** to see their
  **upcoming appointments** and whether **results are on file**.

A back button returns to the choice screen at any point.

<Callout type="warn" title="Privacy by design">
  The kiosk is unauthenticated, so it shows the **minimum** necessary. Both flows require a
  matching **name *and* file number**, and the results view only tells the patient *whether*
  results exist (and how many) — never the clinical values themselves. It asks them to have
  a staff member review results with them. For a hardened deployment, put the kiosk on a
  dedicated device and consider a per-kiosk token or one-time verification code.
</Callout>

Behind the scenes the kiosk talks to the public [Patient Portal API](/docs/api/portal).
