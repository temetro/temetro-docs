When you sign in, temetro opens on the **chat**. It works like any messaging app: type
at the bottom, and the conversation appears above.

## Working with patients

### Pulling up a patient

The fastest way to open a chart is the **patient command**. Type a slash, the word
`patient`, and the patient's file number:

```text
/patient 1042
```

A shorter form works too — just the slash and the number:

```text
/1042
```

temetro replies with the patient's **record cards**: a row of cards summarizing
demographics, alerts, vitals, allergies, medications, problems, recent labs, and
encounters. Cards with more behind them show a **Click for more** hint — open one for the
full details in a dialog, where you can also edit the record (if your role allows it).
Cards for empty sections (no medications on file, say) stay quiet and aren't clickable.

### Adding a patient from the chat

Next to the message box there is an **Add patient** button. It opens the new-patient
form directly — you don't have to leave the chat to register someone. The same form is
available on the [Patients page](/docs/guides/patients).

## Asking questions in plain language

Beyond the `/patient` command, you can ask in plain language — *"what were her last three
blood pressure readings?"* or *"show me 10293's labs"*. The chat runs an AI agent that
looks up the right records and replies with **record cards** and **lab charts**, not just
text. Pick the model and effort from the model picker in the input. As it works, a
**Thinking…** indicator and a **step trace** show what the agent is doing — looking up a
patient, searching, or validating records. Each tool the agent runs also appears as a
collapsible **tool card** (its inputs, result, and status), and on models that support
it a **reasoning** block shows the model's thinking.

On a fresh chat, **suggestion chips** offer quick starts ("Show today's schedule",
"List open tasks", …). And if you send another message while the agent is still
responding, it joins a **queue** above the input and sends automatically when the
agent is free — so you can line up follow-ups without waiting.

You bring your own model: connect an **OpenAI, Anthropic, or Gemini** API key, or run a
**local model with Ollama** — all configured under **Settings → AI**. Because this is
patient data, two safeguards are always in play: **Veil** de-identifies records before
anything reaches a cloud provider, and any **add** waits for your approval before it's
written. The first time a message would leave your clinic for a cloud provider, an inline
**Veil** confirmation appears above the input (you can also switch to a local model) — it
asks just once per session. See [AI setup & safeguards](/docs/guides/ai-safeguards).

### Displaying and adding records

Ask to **see** the schedule, open tasks, or prescriptions — *"show today's
appointments"*, *"list open tasks"* — and the agent renders them as list cards.

It can also answer about the **whole clinic**: *"what's my clinic called?"*, your
**analytics and earnings** (*"how much have we billed this month?"* renders KPIs and an
earnings chart), and your **inventory** (*"what's running low?"*). These are clinic
aggregates, not individual charts.

### Attaching files

Use the **+** button by the message box to attach files (CSV, JSON, PDF, images, …). They
show as tidy **attachment chips** in the conversation — not as a wall of pasted text.
Behind the scenes temetro reads text-like files into the prompt so the agent can parse
them, and passes images/PDFs to vision-capable models. You can attach a file whether or
not the box already has text.

### Building an invoice from a file

Hand the chat a list of medications or items a client bought (a CSV/PDF/text export) and
ask it to invoice them. The agent reads the prices from the file, drafts an **invoice**
with line items, and shows it as an approval card — nothing is billed until you **Add** it.
If the client isn't a patient yet, approving it also creates their record. See
[Invoices](/docs/guides/invoices).

### Stocking inventory from a file

Upload a stock or purchase list (names, quantities, optionally prices) and ask temetro to
**add it to inventory**. The agent parses the items and shows an **approval card**; on
**Add** the items are saved to your [inventory](/docs/guides/pharmacy). This is distinct
from invoicing — use it to *stock* the clinic rather than *bill* a patient.

### Your chat history

Conversations are **saved automatically**. Open the **history panel** from the toggle at
the top-left of the chat (next to the sidebar) to browse and search past chats in a side
sheet, and start a fresh one with **Start new chat**. Past chats also appear in the
**sidebar** — click one to reopen it exactly as it was (record cards and all), or delete
it. The sidebar's **Ask temetro** entry always opens a fresh thread. History is private to
you within your clinic.

You can also ask it to **add** things — *"book a follow-up for 10293 next Tuesday at 9:30
with Dr Lee"*, *"add a task to call the lab"*, *"prescribe amoxicillin 500mg twice daily
for 7 days for 10293"*. The agent **proposes** the record as a one-click approval card;
nothing is written until you click **Add**, and the action still respects your
[role's permissions](/docs/admin/roles-permissions). The agent only **displays** and
**adds** — it never edits, deletes, or changes existing records.

When it proposes **many** records at once (for example from an imported file), they're
grouped into a **single approval** instead of one card each: **Review & add** opens a
dialog listing every record where you can remove any you don't want and **Add all** in one
go. Booking an appointment for someone who isn't a patient yet also **creates their patient
record** (with a file number), so they show up on the Patients page.

### Importing an existing database

Attach a database export (CSV/JSON) and ask temetro to import it — a great way to **migrate
from another program**. See [Migrating from another system](/docs/guides/migration) for the
full walkthrough, including export tips for popular EHRs.

## Tips

- File numbers are unique within your clinic — see
  [Core concepts](/docs/concepts#patients-and-file-numbers).
- Everything you can reach from the chat is also available from the sidebar pages
  (Patients, Appointments, Prescriptions, Tasks, Messages, Notes, Activity, Analysis).
- What you can see and edit depends on your [role](/docs/admin/roles-permissions) —
  reception staff, for example, see demographics but not clinical details.
