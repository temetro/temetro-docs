**Temetro Network** is a small, standalone service that connects your clinic to patients'
[wallet apps](/docs/guides/wallet-import). When a clinic imports a record from a phone, pushes an
update, or a patient approves a share, those messages travel through the network.

It is open source and lives in its own repository:
[github.com/temetro/temetro-network](https://github.com/temetro/temetro-network).

## Why it exists

Earlier, the clinic backend talked to phones directly and exposed itself to the internet through a
temporary tunnel. That tunnel was unreliable — it worked sometimes and not others, and a patient
scanning the QR would sometimes see "couldn't reach the clinic."

Temetro Network replaces that tunnel with a dedicated relay at a **stable address** that is always
on. Both your backend and patient phones connect to it, and it routes messages between them.

## What it does (and does not) do

The relay is a **dumb pipe** by design:

- It **forwards** the encrypted messages between your backend and the right phone.
- It **verifies** that a device controls its wallet (a signature check) before routing to it — this
  is the only cryptography it performs.
- It **never** opens sealed record bundles, holds **no database**, and stores nothing. Only your
  clinic backend (which holds the ephemeral key) can decrypt a shared record.

<Callout type="info" title="End-to-end encrypted">
  Records are sealed on the patient's phone and can only be opened by your clinic. The network only
  ever sees ciphertext, so running it — even on someone else's infrastructure — never exposes
  patient data.
</Callout>

## Deploying your own

The network deploys in minutes on [Railway](https://railway.app) or any container host.

1. Create a new project from the
   [temetro-network](https://github.com/temetro/temetro-network) repo. It ships a `Dockerfile` and
   `railway.toml`, so the platform builds it automatically. The health check is `GET /healthz`.
2. Set one service variable, **`RELAY_TOKEN`** — a shared secret. Generate it with
   `openssl rand -base64 32`. (`PORT` is injected by the platform.)
3. Point your clinic backend at the deployed relay by setting these environment variables (see
   [Self-hosting](/docs/admin/self-hosting)):

   | Variable | Value |
   |----------|-------|
   | `RELAY_URL` | the relay's public URL (e.g. `https://your-relay.up.railway.app`) |
   | `RELAY_TOKEN` | the **same** secret you set on the relay |

4. Point the wallet app's relay URL at the same public URL.

Once all three (relay, backend, wallet app) share the URL and token, the whole
[import](/docs/guides/wallet-import) and [update](/docs/guides/wallet-updates) flow runs on
infrastructure you control.

## How a share travels the network

```
  patient phone  ──▶  Temetro Network  ──▶  clinic backend
   (wallet app)          (the relay)        (opens the record)
```

1. The clinic sends an encrypted share request; the relay forwards it to the patient's phone.
2. The patient approves in their wallet. The app seals the record to the clinic's one-time key and
   sends it back through the relay.
3. The relay hands the sealed bundle to your backend, which opens it and shows the draft record for
   the clinician to review and commit.

The relay treats every one of those messages as opaque bytes.
