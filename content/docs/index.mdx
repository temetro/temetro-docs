**temetro** is an open-source tool for clinics. Instead of clicking through menus and
forms to find patient information, you simply *ask* — temetro's chat understands what
you need and answers with organized, easy-to-read **record cards**: vitals, allergies,
medications, labs, encounters, and more.

Everything your clinic does day to day lives in one place:

- **Patient records** — demographics, problems, allergies, medications, labs, and visit history.
- **Appointments, prescriptions, and tasks** — schedule, prescribe, and keep the team on track.
- **Staff messaging** — real-time chat between members of your clinic.
- **Activity & analytics** — a full audit trail of every change, plus a clinic dashboard.

temetro supports **multiple clinics**: each clinic is its own private workspace with its
own patients, staff, and roles. You can belong to several clinics and switch between them.

<Callout type="warn" title="The AI is not connected yet">
  temetro is under active development. The chat interface works — including patient
  lookups with the `/patient` command — but free-form questions currently get a
  **placeholder reply**. Connecting a real AI model is the next item on the
  [roadmap](/docs/roadmap).
</Callout>

## The bigger idea: patient-owned records

Most medical software stores your record in the doctor's database, and you never see
what changes. temetro is being built toward a different model: the patient's record can
live on the **patient's own device**. When a clinician changes something, the change is
**signed** and is not final until the patient **approves it** in a companion app.

This part of temetro is still a vision — it is not built yet. You can read more about it
in [Core concepts](/docs/concepts#patient-owned-records-the-vision) and follow progress
on the [roadmap](/docs/roadmap).
